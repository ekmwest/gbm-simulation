Geometric Brownian Motion Simulation

Would You Play This Game?

You're offered to play a simple game:

  1. You start with one dollar of wealth.
  2. You guess heads or tails.
  3. A coin is flipped.
  4. If you win, your wealth increases with 50%.
  5. If you loose, your wealth decreases with 40%.
  6. Repeat 2-5.

Seems like a favourably game, right? Would you play it?

Answer

No, you should not play this game.

Why?

On average, you'll loose.

Explanation

An average player will gain 5% wealth for each coin toss: (1.5 + 0.6) / 2 = 1.05

Here's a simulation of 10 000 players making 10 rounds. Vertical axis displays average wealth (log scale)

However, you're not an average player. You're a single player and will, on average, loose 10% wealth for each coin toss: 1.5 * 0.6 = 0.9

Here's a simulation of a single player making 10 000 rounds. Vertical axis displays wealth (log scale):

What?!

The game is non-ergodic. This means that the ensemble average (the averge of many players) is different from the time average (one player over time).
